# Host I — The Operations Optimizer

## Profile

- **Portfolio:** 4-5 units, LOW-CONFIDENCE suburban [40, 46]
- **Years hosting:** Multi-year [46]
- **Channels:** Airbnb [46]
- **Tech stack:** Schlage Encode, RemoteLock software, Airbnb integration [40, 46]
- **Turnover frequency:** High volume (almost entirely 2 to 3-night stays) [47]
- **Check-in handling:** Cleaners act as backup for check-in [48]

## Specific incident history

Guests repeatedly complained about the lock keypad because the numbers remain completely invisible until touched [49]. Guests would press a random spot to wake the screen, which the lock registered as the first digit of the code, causing them to fail entry and get locked out [49, 50].

## Current workaround

The host guarantees the porch light is always on via a timer, relies on housekeepers who have their own codes to assist guests, and provides highly detailed manual instruction cards [48, 49].

## Emotional posture

Reluctant adopter dealing with constant user-error [49, 51].

## What they want

A smart lock that is immediately intuitive for guests who have never used one before, without requiring extra steps to activate the screen [49, 51].

## What they refuse to pay for

Replacing their entire hardware stack when providing a simple instruction card solves the user-error problem [18].

## Decision criteria for replacement / new lock

Physical buttons instead of touchscreens to eliminate "wake up" errors and improve visibility [49, 52].

Airhosts Forum, Reddit [18, 40, 46-52]
