# Host I — The Operations Optimizer (synthesis)

## Anonymized profile

Multi-year suburban STR host. Four active units, all Airbnb, all clustered in one geographic pocket. Schlage Encode hardware on every door, RemoteLock as the software layer for booking-to-code automation. Solo operator with two rotating housekeeping teams as her real check-in safety net. Medium tech savviness, reluctant adopter. Already paying for a paid software layer on top of Schlage hardware.

## Top 3 pains

1. **Touchscreen keypad invisibility.** Numbers don't appear until the screen is touched, and the wake-up press gets registered as the first digit of the code. "Guests... thinking it's four, and the lock obviously rejects it. This happens, I'd say, twice a month at least." This is the dominant pain and no software can fix it.
2. **No proactive signal of readiness.** RemoteLock confirms a code was pushed but not that the guest got in. "I find out when somebody texts 'the code isn't working' and my stomach drops." Low-grade Friday-night anxiety is a recurring cost.
3. **Review-ranking exposure from bad check-ins.** "One bad first-thirty-minutes experience and you're getting a four-star with a comment about 'confusing entry' and that knocks your search ranking." Dollar cost is small; ranking cost is what scares her.

## Reactions per capability

- **Pre-arrival readiness check:** Strongest positive. "It's solving the silence." This is the part she'd pay for; everything else is bundled.
- **Code lifecycle automation:** Indifferent. RemoteLock already does this. Treats it as commodity.
- **Cleaner code rotation:** Skeptical. Her cleaners have permanent codes by design and she'd cut this from v1. Will not trust auto-revoke in the first six months without an opt-in toggle.
- **No remote unlock:** Welcomed. "Keeping me out of the unlock loop is actually a feature."
- **No guest messaging:** Welcomed. Airbnb owns the relationship; Schlage stepping in would confuse guests.
- **No after-stay surveillance:** Welcomed. Doesn't want the privacy headache.
- **Notification preferences:** Push notification only, batched across units, two hours pre-arrival. Not email, not SMS. Wants specific verbs in failure alerts ("battery at 12 percent, replace before 4pm"), not generic "lock issue."

## Standout quotes

- "The lock isn't the system, I am the system, and the lock is supposed to let me not be the system."
- "I have to assume it's fine. And then a guest texts and the assumption breaks."
- "If the green light lies, the green light is worse than no green light, because now I'm relaxed when I should be tense."
- "You're not adding a tool to my stack, you're displacing one."

## Van Westendorp pricing (total monthly, 4-unit portfolio)

| Price point | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Too expensive | $60+/mo |
| Expensive but considering | ~$40/mo |
| Great deal | $25/mo |
| Too cheap (suspicious) | <$10/mo |

Anchor: she pays ~$30/mo for RemoteLock today. Anything under that with the readiness check included is "a no-brainer."

## Killer objection

**"It doesn't fix the keypad."** The readiness check confirms software state (battery, WiFi, code synced, code on lock) but cannot resolve the physical UX failure mode that drives most of her actual lockouts. The green light could be valid and the guest still can't see the buttons. Until Schlage addresses keypad visibility at the hardware layer, this product patches the wrong layer for her use case.

## What surprised us

- **She'd cancel RemoteLock, not stack the product on top.** We expected augmentation; she gave us displacement.
- **The "guest got in" confirmation is the feature she actually wants** and it's not in scope. Readiness signals system state; she wants signal of human success.
- **Cleaner rotation is a "cut from v1" call**, not a v1 differentiator. Long-term cleaners with permanent codes is the operating norm, not the exception.
- **She'd run the product in parallel with RemoteLock for 30 days before switching.** Trust is earned by matching reality, not by marketing.

## RemoteLock displacement signal

**Verdict: REPLACE, at the right price.** This persona is the clearest displacement case in the cohort so far. RemoteLock costs her roughly $30/mo for code automation she now considers commodity. Schlage AI Access Concierge bundles that automation with the readiness check she actually wants. At her "great deal" point of $25/mo total, she cancels RemoteLock outright. At her "expensive" point of $40/mo, she still considers it but RemoteLock retention becomes a real risk for the competitor and a real win for Schlage. Above $60/mo, she stays on RemoteLock and Schlage loses the wallet entirely.

Positioning implication: do not pitch this as a Schlage add-on. Pitch it as a RemoteLock replacement for Schlage hardware owners, with the readiness check as the unique value. The displacement-economics framing should be in the appendix as a defensible pricing anchor: any monthly price at or below the prevailing RemoteLock spend (~$25-30/mo for a 4-unit portfolio) converts this segment.
