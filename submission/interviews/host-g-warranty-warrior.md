# Host G — The Warranty Warrior (Synthesis)

## Anonymized profile

Six-plus years hosting, four to five units across two markets, Airbnb-only. Operates solo with two rotating cleaning crews and an automation-heavy stack. Thirteen Schlage Encode locks installed over time. High tech savviness. Existing customer at scale, burned by hardware failures and an expired warranty, currently uses physical-key lockboxes as a permanent fallback even on smart-lock doors.

## Top 3 pains

1. **Catastrophic hardware failure with no warranty recourse.** A lock died at six months, the warranty replacement also failed mid-check-in, and out-of-warranty replacement cost real money on a calendar technicality. *"Two hundred and twenty-five dollars, out of pocket, because of a calendar."*
2. **Battery percentage you cannot trust.** Linear drain to 50%, then a cliff. Drives constant app-checking and premature battery swaps. *"You can't trust the percentage."*
3. **Guest as failure-detector.** The app shows green while the lock is bricked, so the first signal of trouble is always a guest message. *"That message is the worst sound in the world."*

## Reactions per capability

- **Pre-arrival readiness check:** Highest-value piece by a wide margin. Wants the battery percentage in the ready message, not just a green check, because they have stopped trusting status indicators.
- **"Ready" notification:** Push, short, unit-named, with battery number and signal state. No bundling across units.
- **"Not ready" notification:** Must include unit, specific failure mode, time-to-check-in, and the next action. Vague alerts will cause panic and erode trust faster than no alert.
- **Code automation (guest):** Nice-to-have, partly redundant with their existing integration.
- **Cleaner-code rotation:** Wants a confirmation step on day one, fully automatic only after a long trust-building window. *"The moment I trust it fully is the moment it betrays me."*
- **No remote unlock (anti-goal):** Strongly endorsed. Fewer parties with unlock authority is a feature.
- **No in-stay monitoring (anti-goal):** Pushed back. Wants at least a mid-stay battery check on multi-night bookings, because dead-mid-stay is a real failure mode the product currently ignores.
- **No guest messaging (anti-goal):** Strongly endorsed. Their messaging is dialed in and branded.
- **No auto-extend (anti-goal):** Strongly endorsed. Money decision belongs to the host.

## Standout quotes

- *"The lockboxes never went away, they just got demoted."*
- *"This is software riding on top of the same lock that died on me at six months. If the lock fails, the software is irrelevant."*
- *"Get the check right, prove it works for ninety days, then earn the right to touch my codes."*
- *"The relationship-saving move is what you do when the system is wrong."*

## Killer objection

*"Why am I paying you a monthly fee to monitor the hardware that you sold me, which fails outside of warranty, which you then refuse to stand behind?"* The subscription is read as a second charge for an already-broken promise. Their proposed unlock: bundle extended hardware warranty into the subscription. Without that, monthly pricing is a non-starter for this segment regardless of software quality.

## What surprised us

1. **Good software alone does not rescue Schlage's reputation with this customer.** We expected the AI Access Concierge concept to land as a redemption arc. Instead, it landed as a polish job that intensifies a perceived double-billing problem. The hardware-warranty grievance is load-bearing for purchase intent in a way the concept does not address.
2. **The in-stay anti-goal is contested for this segment.** Hosts who have lived mid-stay lock-death don't want one check before arrival. They want at least a battery ping during multi-night stays. This is the only anti-goal this persona pushed back on, and the reasoning is grounded in real failure history.
3. **The recovery experience matters as much as the primary experience.** The persona spent more emotional energy describing what they wanted to happen *when the product is wrong* (automated apology, credit, log transparency within an hour) than describing the happy path. For this segment, post-failure UX is the brand trust mechanism.

## Retention signal

**Conditional and shallow.** This customer would try the product because they want the pre-arrival check, but the software alone will not retain them on Schlage hardware. Their next lock purchase decision is still gated on warranty quality, not software features. To convert this segment from "trying it" to "loyal," the subscription has to carry warranty coverage with it, and the recovery experience after a false-ready event has to be visibly better than what Schlage support has historically offered. Absent both, expect this segment to adopt the software, keep the lockbox in place, and quietly evaluate Yale and RemoteLock for the next refresh cycle.
