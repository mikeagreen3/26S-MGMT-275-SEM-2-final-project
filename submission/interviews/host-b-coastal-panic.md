# Host B — The Coastal Panic (Synthesis)

## Anonymized profile

Newer Airbnb host (under two years) running a single beach unit roughly 90 minutes from where they live. Solo operator with one local cleaner and no co-host or PMS. One Schlage Encode on the front door, installed about 18 months ago. Low-to-moderate tech savviness. Hardware failure under coastal weather is the dominant pain. Trusted information channel is BiggerPockets STR forum, not pro communities.

## Top 3 pains (with quotes)

1. **Hardware death under weather, with the host 90 minutes away.** A storm caused water intrusion on both Encodes. A family with an infant was stuck on the porch in the rain for 90 minutes while the host drove out. "I'll never forget it. I was making dinner and my phone rang and I could hear the wind through the phone."
2. **Backup-key discipline failures compounding hardware failures.** The lockbox was empty the day of the incident because the host had forgotten to replace the spare. "The smart lock is the front door. The lockbox is the parachute. Don't touch my parachute."
3. **Battery and code-list anxiety as a constant low-grade tax.** "I look at the battery percentage probably twice a day. I'm not exaggerating. Twice a day. It's a tic at this point." Also: 14+ stale codes sitting in the app from forgotten cleanups.

## Reactions per capability

- **Pre-arrival readiness check:** Highest-value capability by a wide margin. "Pre-arrival check. By a mile." Wants it as SMS, not in-app. Two-to-three hours before check-in feels right.
- **"Ready" message:** Short, named, one line. "Beach house is ready for the Smiths, 3pm check-in." Strongly anti-paragraph.
- **"Not ready" message:** Must state the specific failure plus the action. Will lose trust fast if the alert is vague. "Do not send me a 'not ready' alert that just says 'something's wrong, check the app.'"
- **Guest code automation (create at booking, revoke at checkout):** Accepted, useful. Solves the stale-code pileup.
- **Cleaner code rotation:** Rejected for v1. Long-tenured cleaner with a permanent code; auto-revoke creates a turnover catastrophe risk. Wants opt-in or off by default.
- **No remote unlock (anti-goal):** Endorsed without prompting. Frames it as a security positive.
- **No direct guest messaging (anti-goal):** Endorsed. Sees guest comms as their personal brand, not Schlage's.
- **No after-stay surveillance (anti-goal):** Surprised it was even a question. Strongly anti-surveillance. "That feels creepy."
- **No auto-extension (anti-goal):** Endorsed. Wants to own the money/trust call themselves.

## Standout quotes

- "My failure mode is hardware. My locks have died from weather. This doesn't stop that. It just gives me a head start."
- "I'd rather have no system and check it myself than a system that lied to me. Lying is worse than not knowing."
- "Sell me the lock that doesn't die. Then we can talk about the software."
- "The smart lock is the front door. The lockbox is the parachute."

## Van Westendorp four-point pricing (USD/month, single property)

- **Too expensive to consider:** $40
- **Expensive but would think about it:** $25
- **Great deal:** $10
- **So cheap I'd question it:** $3

## Killer objection

"Your software update is not going to keep my locks alive in a storm. You're telling me earlier that they're dying, but you're not stopping it. Why am I paying you a monthly fee for an earlier warning when what I actually need is a lock that survives the coast?" The Concierge is positioned as a SaaS layer on top of hardware that, for this segment, is the actual point of failure. Without a hardware story (weatherized SKU, moisture sensing, ruggedization roadmap), the monthly fee feels like a tax on a problem Schlage caused.

## What surprised us

- **The lockbox is sacred.** We expected smart-lock hosts to be quietly trying to phase out the physical key. This persona is the opposite. The lockbox is psychological infrastructure, and any product story that implicitly retires it will scare them off. There's a positioning lesson: lean into "the Concierge is a second pair of eyes, not a replacement for your backup."
- **Cleaner rotation is a trust hazard, not a feature.** We expected long-tenured-cleaner relationships to be neutral on rotation. They're actively hostile. The auto-revoke is what scares them, not the convenience that wins them. v1 should default cleaner rotation off.
- **They want SMS, not an app.** Strong preference for the "ready" message to arrive as a text. Opening an app to see "everything is fine" feels like work. This contradicts our default assumption that the host dashboard is the primary surface.
- **"Lying is worse than not knowing."** A false-positive ready message is worse than no system at all. The accuracy bar on the readiness check is higher than the team has been pricing in. One bad false positive in the first 30 days probably kills the account.
- **They asked about moisture sensing unprompted.** Adjacent product opportunity that sits outside the C-pre scope but came up naturally as a paid upgrade they'd actually want.
