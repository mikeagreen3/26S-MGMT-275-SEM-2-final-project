# Host A — The Frustrated App Wrestler (Synthesis)

## Anonymized profile

Solo operator, single urban unit, almost two years on Airbnb. Schlage Encode on the front door with a Wi-Fi bridge, mesh 2.4/5 GHz network at home. No PMS, no property manager, no co-host. Weekly turnover. One regular cleaner with a permanent code. Trusts Reddit (specifically r/airbnb_hosts) over brand marketing. Medium tech savviness, low patience for the existing lock app, high baseline anxiety on check-in days.

## Top 3 pains

1. **The app lies about lock state.** The single most cited pain. Status indicators (battery, code sync, online status) do not match physical reality, so the host cannot rely on the dashboard to predict success.
   > "The lock said 85 percent battery in the app. Eighty-five. The guests show up and the keypad won't light up at all."

2. **Pre-arrival uncertainty bleeds into sleep and weeks, not just incidents.** Even when nothing fails, the host is refreshing the app multiple times before every arrival.
   > "Even on a normal Friday I'm refreshing the app three times before a guest arrives and I still don't actually know if the code is on the lock until they tell me they got in. It's this low-level dread thing."

3. **Recovery is manual, slow, and expensive.** A single failure runs roughly 40 minutes, costs a star on the review, and forces guests to do battery swaps themselves.
   > "Four-star review. They were nice about it in the message but the stars don't lie."

## Reactions per capability

**Pre-arrival check.** Strongest positive response of the interview, contingent on the check actually pinging the lock rather than reading app state. Once the host confirmed the check was a real device-side ping, the framing shifted from skepticism to genuine relief ("I sleep on Thursday night. That's how it changes."). This is the single capability the host believes they cannot replicate themselves. Critical dependency: it has to work on a mesh 2.4/5 GHz network without manual band-splitting, or "dead on arrival."

**Code automation.** Useful, not transformative. The host has a workable copy-paste habit and would rather see effort go into the readiness check. They would accept it, but it isn't the reason they buy.

**Cleaner rotation.** Hard pushback. The cleaner has had the same code for almost two years and is treated as part of the operating infrastructure, not a rotating contractor. The host wants this opt-in, off by default, with day-before notice and a per-person pause. There is real risk of a "Tuesday rotation, Wednesday lockout" scenario the host cannot fix from a meeting. The honest answer: they would probably leave it off entirely.

**Anti-goals.** Mostly endorsed, with one nuance. No remote unlock ("keep it out"), no after-stay surveillance ("now I have a problem I didn't have"), and no auto-extending codes ("I want to make that call") all landed cleanly. The "no guest messaging" anti-goal got conditional pushback: if Schlage ever did build it, the host would want every message and every guest reply mirrored back to them, because Airbnb is the system of record for disputes.

## Standout quotes

- "The app is the broken part. If your new product is built on top of the same app that lies to me about battery levels, why is bolting AI on top going to fix that?"
- "I sleep on Thursday night. That's how it changes."
- "If I get an alert that says 'lock issue detected' and nothing else I will scream."
- "I'd go back to Reddit before I'd believe any of this."

## Van Westendorp four-point pricing

| Point | Price | One-line rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Too expensive | $30/mo | "If you told me thirty I'd close the tab." Single-unit host with no PMS, no margin tolerance for a lock add-on. |
| Expensive but considering | $15/mo | Would sanity-check on Reddit before committing; pricing here triggers research, not purchase. |
| Great deal | $7/mo | Netflix-low subscription threshold; clears the bar without deliberation. |
| Too cheap (suspect) | Under $3/mo | Triggers a "what's the catch / am I the product" reaction. |

## Killer objection

"Why should I believe this app is going to work when the current app is the whole reason I'm in this mess? Fix the app first."

## What surprised us

The host enthusiastically embraced the "no remote unlock" and "no after-stay surveillance" anti-goals as features rather than tolerating them as gaps, framing both as risk-reduction. The cleaner-rotation pushback was sharper than expected: trust in the system collapses entirely once it touches the relationship with a long-tenured cleaner, even though the same host accepted full guest-code automation without hesitation.
