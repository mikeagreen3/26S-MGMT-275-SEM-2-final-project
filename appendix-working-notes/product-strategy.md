## Part A: Context & Vision

**Company Mission:** The front door is the entry point every smart home system routes through, and still the place where connected access most often fails. Allegion's mission is "pioneering safety" through seamless access. Schlage carries over a century of BHMA AAA-rated hardware credibility (founded 1920) that software-first competitors cannot shortcut. Schlage's connected lock lineup extends this into the smart home: retrofit-ready Wi-Fi deadbolts requiring no hub, for users who want connected access without sacrificing the reassurance of a real lock.

**Key Industry Trends:** Three structural trends are reshaping the category. First, the industry's move to Matter/Thread (Silicon Labs, 2024) has made interoperability a table-stakes feature. Second, AI advances are enabling non-technical users to configure and monitor smart home devices through natural language, expanding the addressable market beyond tech-forward early adopters. Third, the short-term rental (STR) economy (5M+ active Airbnb hosts globally, Airbnb Q4 2024 Earnings Release) has created a structurally different buyer who needs operations-grade reliability from a consumer product. A lock failure for a homeowner is an inconvenience. For a host with guests arriving, it is an operational emergency. That asymmetry creates willingness to pay the traditional DIY retrofit segment never had.

**Key Technical Insights:** The failures that frustrate smart lock users are software and infrastructure problems, not hardware ones. Two technical shifts are changing that. First, Thread's low-power, mesh-based local processing moves security logic on-device, eliminating the home network instability that Wi-Fi locks currently inherit. Second, AI and ML unlock two distinct capabilities: predictive failure detection from sensor patterns before devices go offline, and intelligent access orchestration that manages time-bounded credentials, anticipates guest arrivals, and serves as the first touchpoint in the rental experience.

**Competitive Landscape:** The North American smart lock market was $1.09B in 2024, projected to reach $2.72B by 2030 at a 19.7% CAGR (Grand View Research, 2025). Yale Assure Lock 2 is the closest hardware challenger but has no meaningful software roadmap. August's convenience-only positioning cannot serve the STR segment: trust is non-negotiable when a host's guests can't get in, and August cannot buy a century of security credibility. Aqara and similar entrants are ecosystem specialists for Apple-centric households, real competitors for a narrow segment but without Schlage's cross-platform breadth. No competitor combines hardware trust, cross-platform ecosystem reach, and a software roadmap for the STR segment. That is the gap Schlage should own.

**Hypothesis About Winning:** Schlage's winning position rests on three resources competitors cannot quickly replicate. First, brand and distribution: Schlage is the default security brand in US retail, with BHMA AAA certification and shelf presence at Home Depot and Lowe's that no app-first competitor has cleared. Second, installed base: a large network of connected smart deadbolts and levers generates access behavior data (failure patterns, battery degradation curves, connectivity anomalies) that can train predictive models no new entrant can match at scale. Investing in data infrastructure to convert that installed base into a software moat, before Yale or August does, is the strategic imperative. Feature parity is acquirable. A trusted brand, in the right retail channels, backed by a behavioral dataset is not.
